Welcome to Fished it, Mate!


A community-driven blog dedicated to all fishing enthusiasts! Whether you're a seasoned angler or just starting out, this platform is designed for like-minded individuals who share a passion for fishing. Here, we celebrate the joys of casting lines, reeling in catches, and sharing our unique experiences on the water. Our blog is a gathering place to help 'log' your sessions and to add stories, tips, and discussions about everything related to fishing. From the thrill of that days catch to the serenity of the great outdoors, we invite you to create your own posts, explore other posts and connect with fellow fishing lovers. Each blog post can be searched, and also provided in an archive in your own profile, offering insights into various types of fish caught, and methods used, and fishing locations by our community members.

How It Works

At Fished it, Mate!, we believe in making information accessible and engaging. Here's how you can navigate our blog:

Creat a profile: The first thing to do is register and create a profile, this allows you to create posts and keep track of your fishing information. You can still see posts but won't be able to write your own without a profile.
Type of Fish Caught: Add your favourite fish in your profile, and the ability to add a fish type when you make a blog to discuss your fishing session. Each venue or each session provides a variety of fish to discuss so a dropdown menu makes this easier to add to your posts.
Fishing Method Used: Whether you prefer fly fishing, deep-sea fishing, or float waggler, you can choose the fishing methods that interest you the most in your profile, and again detail this for your sessions in your blog posts.

Join us in celebrating the art of fishing! Share your stories, learn from others, and contribute to a vibrant community that values the beauty of nature and the thrill of the catch. Happy fishing!

Please check for rules and legislation for your own country. If you're planning to fish in UK waters, you're probably going to need a fishing licence.
